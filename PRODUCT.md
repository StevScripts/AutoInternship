# Product

## Register

product

## App Name

AutoInternship

## Users
Stevin George — CS junior at UCF, actively hunting for SWE/AI/ML/DevOps internships across the US. Solo user. Opens the app on his phone (PWA) multiple times daily to review scraped job matches, approve pre-filled applications, and track application status. Also receives Discord digest notifications linking back to the app.

## Product Purpose
Automated internship hunting system that scrapes job boards on a schedule, scores matches against Stevin's resume, pre-fills applications, and waits for manual approval before submitting. Tracks every application's lifecycle — from discovery to submission to recruiter outreach. The goal: always be the most perfect candidate. Zero manual grunt work, full control over what gets submitted.

## Brand Personality
Sharp, Efficient, Relentless

- Voice: direct, no fluff, action-oriented
- Tone: confident operator — this is a machine that works for you
- Emotional goal: feeling of being in control of a powerful system that never sleeps

## Anti-references
- Cluttered job board UIs (Indeed, Monster) — information overload, visual noise, ads everywhere
- Generic SaaS dashboards with purple gradients — the "AI startup landing page" look
- Overly corporate enterprise tools — stiff, lifeless, committee-designed
- Glassmorphism-heavy AI product UIs
- Identical card grids with icon + heading + text repeated endlessly

## Design Principles
1. **Status at a glance** — every screen answers "what happened, what needs my attention, what's next" without scrolling
2. **One tap to act** — review, approve, skip, message — every action is one interaction away
3. **Machine transparency** — show what the system did, why it matched, what it filled. No black boxes.
4. **Density without clutter** — pack information tight (Linear-style) but with clear hierarchy. No wasted space, no visual noise.
5. **Relentless forward motion** — the UI should feel like momentum. Queued jobs, pending approvals, sent applications — always moving.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
No specific requirements. Solo personal use. Can be iterated as needed.
